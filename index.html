Dave Ramsey’s 7 Baby Steps

The proven path to go from broke to millionaire.
2.8M+ views on YouTube — now simplified for teens.

1

Save $1,000 Fast

Your starter emergency fund. Sell stuff, work extra, get it in 30 days max.

Teen Move: Sell old games → $1k in a weekend.

2

Debt Snowball

List debts smallest → largest. Pay minimums + attack the smallest with everything.

Teen Move: Kill $200 phone bill first → roll payment to next.

3

3–6 Months Expenses

Fully funded emergency fund. You’re now the bank — never borrow again.

Teen Goal: Save $3k+ from summer jobs.

4

Invest 15% of Income

Roth IRA + 401(k) match → growth stock mutual funds.

Teen Start: Open Roth at 16 with $50/month.

5

College (No Debt!)

Save for kids’ college. Community college, scholarships, work — no loans.

Teen Hack: Dual-enroll high school → free credits.

6

Pay Off House Early

Chunk extra at mortgage. Average: paid off in 7–8 years.

Future You: Own home by 30.

7

Build Wealth & Give

Millionaire status → give like no one else. Fun + generosity.

Endgame: Pay for little bro’s college in cash.

Stocks & Investing 101

Own a piece of companies you love. Start with $5 on teen-friendly apps.

What is a Stock?

A tiny slice of a company. When the company grows, your slice is worth more.

Types of Stocks

Growth: Tech (Apple, Tesla)
Dividend: Pays you cash (Coca-Cola)
Index Funds: Own the whole market (S&P 500)

How to Read a Quote

Price: $150.32
Change: +2.10 (1.4%)
Volume: 12.4M shares traded

Credit & Debt – The Truth

Credit isn’t free money. Use it right → free trips. Use it wrong → stress.

How Credit Works

Borrow → Pay back on time → Good score.
Late/miss → Score drops → Higher interest.

Credit Score (FICO)

300–850
35% Payment history
30% Amounts owed
15% Length of history

Good vs. Bad Debt

Good: Student loan (future income)
Bad: $800 phone on 24% interest

Teen Tips

Become authorized user on parent card (no spending).
Pay phone bill on time → builds score.
Never borrow >30% of limit.

Money Formulas Every Teen Needs

5 core formulas – teen examples included.

Compound Interest
A = P (1 + r/n)nt
A = final amount
P = starting
r = rate
n = compounds/year
t = years
Teen: $50/mo from 16 at 7% → $149k by 65.
Rule of 72
Years to Double = 72 ÷ Return %
Quick doubling estimate.
Teen: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 yrs → $1k → $2k.
50/30/20 Budget
50% Needs • 30% Wants • 20% Savings
Split take-home pay.
Teen: $800 job → $400 essentials, $240 fun, $160 saved.
Debt Snowball
Pay Minimums + Extra → Smallest First
Psychological wins.
Teen: $200 phone, $50 fine → kill fine first.
Net Worth
Assets - Liabilities = Net Worth
Track yearly.
Teen: $1,200 savings + $500 laptop – $300 owed = $1,400.
